ALTAR OF THE FATHERLAND

Altare della Patria

The **Altar of the Fatherland**, also known as the Vittoriano, is one of the most striking national monuments in Italy.

Rising at the heart of Rome’s Piazza Venezia, it was conceived to honor King Victor Emmanuel II, the first king who unified the country.

Constructed from Brescian marble, the structure features grand stairways and a towering colonnade.

At its center stands the mighty equestrian statue of Victor Emmanuel II, cast in bronze.

The monument serves as a tribute to Italy’s unification and a cultural hub offering panoramic views.

HOW IT WAS BUILT

The Construction of the Altar of the Fatherland began in 1885 and was the vision of architect Giuseppe Sacconi.

Its structure relied heavily on Botticino marble, chosen for its bright, almost luminous appearance.

At the center is the enormous equestrian statue of Victor Emmanuel II, which required an intricate steel framework.

In 1921, the structure gained a new sacred function with the installation of the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier, placed beneath the statue of the goddess Roma.

INSIDE THE MONUMENT

The interior and upper terraces reveal a blend of monumental architecture. Above the central terrace rises the grand Colonnade, a long arcade of soaring Corinthian columns.

Inside, the Museum of Italian Unification displays artifacts that trace the Risorgimento movement.

From the uppermost terraces, known as the Terraces of the Quadrighe, visitors can approach the dramatic twin bronze chariots that crown the monument.

These quadrigas represent Victory guiding a united Italy.

THE STRUCTURE TODAY

Today, the Altar of the Fatherland stands as a national symbol and one of the most visited monuments in Rome.

The monument’s striking appearance, crafted from white Botticino marble, gives it a radiant presence that dominates Piazza Venezia.

It presents a powerful tribute to national unity and independence.

Ongoing preservation work ensures the protection of the monument’s sculptures, bronze quadrigas, and interior halls.

The terraces offer expansive panoramic views over Rome, including the Colosseum and Roman Forum.

It remains an active site for civic and military ceremonies.

FUN FACTS

The monument is built almost entirely from Botticino marble, standing out against Rome’s warm-toned buildings.

During construction, a banquet was famously held inside the hollow belly of the horse of the equestrian statue.

The twin bronze chariots are over ten meters tall.

The eternal flame of the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier is guarded continuously.
