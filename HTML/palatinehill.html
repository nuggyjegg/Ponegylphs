PALATINE HILL

Mons Palatinus

Palatine Hill is one of the most ancient and historically significant areas of Rome, known for its commanding views and imperial ruins.

At the heart of the hill stand the remains of the Domus Flavia and Domus Augustana, magnificent palace complexes built for Emperor Domitian.

Another highlight is the Stadium of Domitian, a large, elongated courtyard once used for private ceremonies.

Nearby, the Farnese Gardens showcase a later Renaissance addition to the hill.

Today, Palatine Hill serves as a vast archaeological park where visitors can walk among palace ruins.

HOW IT WAS BUILT

Palatine Hill is the birthplace of Rome’s earliest settlements, inhabited since the 10th century BC. Tradition links this area to Romulus, who founded the city here in 753 BC.

During the Republican period, wealthy Romans built elegant houses overlooking the Forum. The transformation into a grand imperial complex began in the 1st century AD.

Augustus built his Domus Augusti and the Temple of Apollo, establishing the Palatine as the symbolic heart of imperial power.

In the Renaissance, families such as the Farnese created terraced gardens across the ruins, contributing new layers of history.

INSIDE THE MONUMENT

The interior areas of Palatine Hill reveal parts of ancient imperial residences. Visitors can enter sections of the House of Augustus and the House of Livia, where preserved frescoes show colorful mythological scenes.

Nearby, the remains of the Domus Flavia and Domus Augustana display large interior halls, courtyards, and water features.

At the eastern side stands the Stadium of Domitian, a garden court surrounded by curved porticoes.

The preserved chambers and subterranean corridors reveal the hidden artistic and architectural world that shaped life inside ancient Palatine Hill.

THE STRUCTURE TODAY

Today, Palatine Hill remains one of Rome’s most important archaeological and historical sites, offering a close look at the residences of Rome’s earliest emperors.

The hill preserves large sections of the imperial palaces, including the Domus Flavia and Domus Augustana.

Ongoing conservation protects the hill’s ruins, frescoes, and surrounding greenery, allowing visitors to overlook the Roman Forum and Circus Maximus.

The House of Augustus and the House of Livia remain two of the most significant structures, known for their well preserved wall paintings.

These buildings provide rare insight into early imperial art and architecture.

The Palatine Hill is managed as part of the Roman Forum and Colosseum Archaeological Park, ensuring its preservation for future generations.

FUN FACTS

Palatine Hill is known as the birthplace of Rome, where Romulus is said to have founded the city in 753 BC.

The name “Palatine” comes from the Latin word Palatium, which gave rise to the modern word “palace.”

The Domus Augustana included elegant courtyards and decorative gardens that reflect the refined taste of Roman emperors.

The Stadium of Domitian was not a real stadium but a private imperial garden shaped like one.
